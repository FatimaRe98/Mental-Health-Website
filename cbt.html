Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a widely used and evidence-based therapeutic approach that focuses on the connection between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. It is a short-term, goal-oriented therapy that helps individuals identify and modify negative or unhelpful patterns of thinking and behavior to improve their emotional well-being.

  • Principles: CBT is based on the principle that our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors are interconnected and influence each other. By identifying and modifying these patterns, individuals can experience positive changes in their emotions and behaviors.

  • Collaboration and Active Participation: CBT is a collaborative therapy where the therapist and the individual work together as a team. The individual actively participates in the therapy process, setting goals and implementing strategies for change.

  • Cognitive Restructuring: Cognitive restructuring involves identifying and challenging irrational or negative thoughts and replacing them with more balanced and realistic ones.

  • Behavioral Techniques: CBT incorporates various behavioral techniques to help individuals change maladaptive behaviors and develop healthier coping strategies.

  • Skill-Building: CBT emphasizes the development of practical skills to cope with challenging situations and manage emotions effectively.

  • Time-Limited and Structured: CBT is typically a short-term therapy that is focused and time-limited, promoting efficient progress.
